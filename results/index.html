Results


Observations I

The number of countries tweeting about atheletes, when divided by gender, is startkly different, with 60 countries tweeting about male athletes while only 26 countires tweeted about female athletes, i.e. only 43% of the countries that tweeted about male athletes also tweeted about female athletes. The total number of tweets for female athletes was 36,141 while for male it was 131181, i.e. only 21% of all the tweets were about women. The distribution of tweets based on country is shown below.


The first graph I have presented is study of the sentiment divided by gender. Tweets associated with male athletes showed a dominating 54.7% of neutral sentiment, followed by a 38.6% of positive sentiment and 6.7% of negative sentiment. Following this trend tweets about female athletes too showed 48.1% of positive sentiment, followed by 44.7% of neutral sentiment and finally 7.2% negative sentiment. This initial investigation shows that this data is fairly distributed across genders. But there was a caveat, with Serena Williams announcing her pregnancy, a lot of tweets congratulated her on her motherhood. I took out data for Serena Williams (who is the highest paid female athlete in the world) and new relations came to light with negative tweets regarding female athletes increasing to 12.0% (4.8% increase) reducing both positive and neutral tweets to 40.8% and 47.2% respectively. For comparision I filtered out tweets for Cristiano Ronaldo who is the highest paid male athlete, which introduced a smaller change of only 1.1% in negative tweets, i.e. the new data-set had only 7.8% negative tweets and 36.9 positive tweets and 55.2% neutral tweets. This highlights the harsher criticism towards female athletes than their male counterparts as well as higher praise.

The second graph I present is a study of sentiment of tweets athletes further classified by region. Although the total number of tweets regarding female and male athletes was nearly alike, there were 8 countries predominantly tweeting about female athletes while there were 18 countries tweeting about their male counterparts. USA tweeted the highest number of tweets for female athletes, combining positive, neutral and negative tweets. Brasil on the other hand had the highest number of tweets for male athletes with all of them neutral.
